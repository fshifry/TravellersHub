A guide to the Sri Lankan cuisine

Breakfast

Roast Paan (Roasted Bread)

crispy outside and fluffy inside typical srilankan roast paan is ready to be enjoyed with coconut sambal, milky potato curry or anything you like, or simply as it is with a hot cup of tea.Roast Paan is a bread that is baked not as a whole loaf but in slices, which makes it easier to just peel off and eat. Super soft it is the perfect carrier to spicy curries

Pol Roti (Coconut Flat Bread)

A very Sri Lankan recipe for coconut roti. Very quick and easy and a meal in itself with a pat of butter and some onion sambol. Mainly eaten for breakfast but also for dinner. Don't overcook the roti or they will get hard and crispy. Serve hot.
